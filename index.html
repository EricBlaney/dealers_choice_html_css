Holds at the climbing gym

Volume
Volumes are always considered to be "on" for routes near them. They mimic natural environmental features for the routes that utilize them.
Crimp
A small, thin, grip. Commonly footholds. These can be difficult to hold and require a closed grip.
Pocket
Rounded holds with a small space that can fit from one to three fingers. Utilize your strongest fingers to power through a climb. Try and use as many fingers as possible.
Sloper
Friction is the name of the game. You won't be able to grip these holds. Use a flat hand, and keep your arm close to the wall, for the most effective hold.
Jug
The favorite hold for a weary climber. Jugs provide an excellent deep "jug" like grip for climbers to hold on to. Advanced climbs will rarely find jugs en route.
Pinch
Vertically oriented holds that require a pinching motion to hold on to. The pinch can range from including all of a climber's fingers or just two or three.
